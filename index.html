Formula 1

Formula 1 is the highest class of international racing for open-wheel single-seater formula racing cars. It is sanctioned by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA) and has been one of the premier forms of racing around the world since 1950. The word formula refers to the set of rules that all participants and cars must follow. A Formula 1 season consists of a series of races, called Grands Prix, that take place in different countries and continents on either purpose-built circuits or closed public roads. The drivers and the teams compete for two annual World Championships: one for the drivers and one for the teams (constructors). Formula 1 cars are the fastest regulated road-course racing cars in the world, owing to their high-performance engines and aerodynamics. They can reach speeds of over 300 km/h and generate large amounts of downforce that help them corner faster. Formula 1 is also a global business that attracts millions of fans, sponsors, media and broadcasters.
