2023 Teams

RedBull Racing

RedBull Racing is a Formula One team that competes in the FIA World Championship. The team is based in Milton Keynes, UK, and is owned by the energy drink company Red Bull. RedBull Racing has won four constructors' and drivers' titles, with Sebastian Vettel as its most successful driver. The team's current drivers are Max Verstappen and Sergio Perez, who drive the RB19 car powered by Honda engines. RedBull Racing is known for its innovative design, aggressive racing style and partnership with luxury footwear brand APL - Athletic Propulsion Labs .

  • Max Verstappen
  • Sergio Perez
  • 123 PTS

Aston Martin

Aston Martin Formula 1 team is a British racing team based in Silverstone, United Kingdom. The team competes in the F1 World Championship with Mercedes power units and a chassis named AMR23. The team's drivers are Fernando Alonso, a two-time world champion, and Lance Stroll, a young Canadian talent. Aston Martin Formula 1 team aims to challenge for world titles with its ambitious and innovative approach.

  • Fernando Alonso
  • Lance Stroll
  • 65 PTS

Mercedes AMG Petronas

Mercedes AMG Formula 1 team is a professional racing team that competes in the Formula 1 World Championship. The team is based in Brackley, UK, and is owned by Mercedes-Benz and AMG. The team has won seven consecutive constructors' and drivers' titles from 2014 to 2020, making it one of the most successful teams in Formula 1 history. The team's current drivers are Lewis Hamilton and George Russell.

  • Lewis Hamilton
  • George Russell
  • 56 PTS

Ferrari

Scuderia Ferrari is the oldest and most successful team in Formula 1 racing, having competed in every season since 1950. The team has won 16 world championships and 243 races, with legendary drivers such as Alberto Ascari, Niki Lauda and Michael Schumacher. Ferrari is based in Maranello, Italy and uses its own power unit for its cars. The current drivers are Charles Leclerc and Carlos Sainz, who are aiming to challenge for the title in 2023.

  • Charles Leclerc
  • Carlos Sainz Jr.
  • 26 PTS

McLaren

McLaren is one of the oldest and most successful teams in Formula 1 history. Founded by Bruce McLaren in 1963, the team has won 183 races, 12 Drivers' Championships and 8 Constructors' Championships with legendary drivers such as Emerson Fittipaldi, James Hunt, Niki Lauda, Alain Prost, Ayrton Senna, Mika Hakkinen and Lewis Hamilton. McLaren is based in Woking, United Kingdom and currently uses Mercedes power units. The team is led by Andrea Stella and has Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri as its drivers for the 2023 season.

  • Lando Norris
  • Oscar Piastri
  • 12 PTS

Alpine

Alpine F1 Team is a French Formula One constructor that belongs to Renault, a French car manufacturer. The team was rebranded from Renault F1 Team in 2021 to promote Alpine, Renault's sports car brand. The team has two bases: one in Enstone, UK, where the chassis are designed and developed, and one in Viry-Châtillon, France, where the engines are produced. The team's drivers for 2023 are Esteban Ocon and Pierre Gasly, both from France. The team has won two world championships and 21 races in its history as Renault F1 Team.

  • Pierre Gasly
  • Esteban Ocon
  • 8 PTS

Haas

Haas Formula 1 team is an American racing team that competes in the FIA Formula One World Championship. It was founded by Gene Haas, a successful businessman and owner of Haas Automation, a CNC machine tool manufacturer. The team made its debut in 2016 and became the first all-American-led F1 team in three decades. The team is based in Kannapolis, North Carolina, and also has a UK factory in Banbury. The team uses Ferrari engines and has Kevin Magnussen and Nico Hulkenberg as its drivers for the 2023 season. The team's best results so far are a pole position at the 2022 Brazilian Grand Prix and a fourth-place finish at the 2018 Austrian Grand Prix.

  • Kevin Magnussen
  • Nico Hulkenberg
  • 7 PTS
